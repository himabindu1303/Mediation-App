More about Binaural Sounds

Binaural Sounds in Meditation are a type of auditory illusion used to enhance mental states, often applied in mindfulness practices, meditation, and relaxation. The concept of binaural beats is based on the idea that when two different sound frequencies are played in each ear, the brain perceives a third frequency, which is the difference between the two tones. This perceived frequency is known as a binaural beat.

How Binaural Beats Work

Two Different Frequencies: For binaural beats to be effective, two tones with slightly different frequencies are played, one in each ear. For example, if a tone of 300 Hz is played in one ear and a tone of 310 Hz in the other ear, the brain will perceive a 10 Hz beat (the difference between the two frequencies).

Brainwave Synchronization: The brain then "synchronizes" with the frequency of the binaural beat, a process known as brainwave entrainment. This means that the brain adjusts its electrical activity to align with the frequency of the beat, which is often associated with different mental states.
